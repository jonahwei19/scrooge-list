A Note on Method · Vol. I

Methodology

How the Scrooge Score is computed, where it sees clearly, and where it does not.

Overview

The Scrooge List is an inverse Forbes ledger that ranks billionaires by how little of their fortune has been deployed for charity. The aim is transparency: to understand who is giving, who is not, and why the gap exists. The Scrooge Score (0–100) measures philanthropic deployment relative to capacity.

  • Score 0–30: Generous givers — a significant portion of wealth deployed.
  • Score 30–60: Moderate — some observable giving.
  • Score 60–80: Light — minimal relative to capacity.
  • Score 80–100: Scrooge territory — very little observable giving.

The Formula

Score = 100 × (1 − Giving Rate / Benchmark) where:
  Giving Rate = Total Observable Giving ÷ Liquid Wealth
  Benchmark = 10 % (expected lifetime deployment)

• Giving Rate ≥ 10 % → Score = 0 (excellent philanthropist)
• Giving Rate = 0 % → Score = 100 (maximum Scrooge)
• Giving Pledge fulfilled → reward applied
• Giving Pledge broken → penalty applied
• Red flags → additional penalty each

Score Components

  1. Giving Rate (primary factor): Observable charitable deployment divided by deployable wealth.
  2. Liquidity Adjustment: 20–70 % of net worth assumed deployable, varying by wealth source.
  3. Pledge Penalty: Giving Pledge signers lagging commitment carry extra weight.
  4. Red-Flag Penalties: Added per flag (capped at four).

What We Cannot See

Important Limitation A significant portion of billionaire philanthropy flows through opaque channels no public register covers. A high Scrooge Score does not prove a person is ungenerous — it may only mean their giving is invisible.

Channels with zero or minimal transparency:

  • Donor-Advised Funds: $251B+ held, no disclosure of individual accounts. Contributions are deductible immediately; grants to charities may be delayed indefinitely.
  • Dynasty Trusts: $360B+ in South Dakota alone, no public registry. Can operate for centuries.
  • Philanthropic LLCs: CZI, Emerson Collective, Ballmer Group — no 990 filing requirement. We rely on media coverage.
  • Foreign Giving: Overseas donations rarely disclosed. Cross-border philanthropy is essentially invisible.
  • Religious Giving: Churches exempt from 990. Tithing is not publicly tracked.
  • Anonymous Giving: Many gifts are deliberately anonymous. We track only named gifts.

Giving Pledge Analysis

The Giving Pledge is a moral commitment (not legally binding) to give away a majority (> 50 %) of one's wealth. We track:

  • Sign Year — when the pledge was made.
  • Net Worth at Signing — baseline for the commitment.
  • Observable Giving — what we can verify has been deployed.
  • Fulfillment Rate — Observable ÷ (50 % of NW at signing).
Pledge Status Categories
  • Fulfilled: ≥ 100 % of commitment observable.
  • On Track: 50–99 % — good progress.
  • Behind: 10–49 % — some giving, below expectation.
  • Minimal: < 10 % — very little progress.

Data Quality Indicators

  • High: 3+ verified sources (990s, SEC filings, major announced gifts).
  • Medium: 1–2 sources.
  • Low: Estimates only.
  • Estimated: Statistical models based on peer patterns.

On Estimation

For billionaires without verified giving data, we do not fabricate numbers. Instead we:

  1. Query ProPublica for foundations matching the name.
  2. Search for announced gifts in news and Wikipedia.
  3. Check Giving Pledge signer status.
  4. If nothing is found, mark the entry as "Estimated" with no observable giving.

This means those with no public philanthropic record show $0 observable and a high score. This may be unfair to private givers — but we prefer accuracy to fabrication.

Limitations & Disclaimers

  • This is a research project, not a definitive ranking of generosity.
  • Data reflects the last pipeline run — recent gifts may be missing.
  • Many may give through channels we cannot track.
  • Pledge fulfillment is computed vs. NW at signing, not current NW.
  • Name matching is imperfect — some foundations may be missed.
  • International billionaires receive far less US-based transparency.

Data Pipeline

The list is generated by an automated pipeline that:

  1. Fetches the Forbes Real-Time Billionaires list (3,000+).
  2. Queries each of the 16 data-source modules.
  3. Deduplicates findings across categories.
  4. Computes Scrooge Scores.
  5. Publishes to this web application.
